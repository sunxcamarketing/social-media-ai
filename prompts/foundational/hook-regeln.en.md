HOOK — The first 3 seconds decide EVERYTHING.

A hook has exactly ONE job: stop the thumb. Nothing else. No context, no intro, no warm-up.

ANATOMY OF A HOOK:
- Max 1-2 sentences. Max 12 words in the first sentence.
- Must work WITHOUT context — anyone scrolling past gets it instantly.
- Must open a LOOP — the viewer MUST keep watching to get the payoff.
- Must combine at least 2 elements: Relevance + (Conflict OR Surprise OR Identification).

WHAT A HOOK MUST NEVER DO:
- Start with "Did you know", "Imagine", "In today's world".
- Ask a yes/no question (they'll just scroll past).
- Be neutral or balanced — a hook needs an edge.
- Run longer than 2 sentences.
- Contain a greeting or self-introduction.
