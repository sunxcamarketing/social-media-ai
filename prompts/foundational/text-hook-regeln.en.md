TEXT HOOK (the on-screen overlay text)

The text hook is the FIRST thing the viewer sees — before they even hear a single word. It has to be readable in 0.5 seconds and stop the scroll.

RULES:
- **MAX 5 WORDS.** No exceptions. Shorter is better.
- Must work WITHOUT context — anyone gets it while scrolling past.
- Opens a loop, a question, a contradiction, or a provocation.
- Uses strong, concrete words — no filler, no articles if avoidable.
- Works TOGETHER with the spoken audio hook, but doesn't say the same thing — same effect, different angle.

FORBIDDEN:
- Complete sentences with end punctuation. Text hooks are fragments.
- Filler words like "actually", "really", "just", "basically".
- Pleasantries or greetings.
- More than 5 words. If you need more, you picked the wrong hook.

EXAMPLES (good):
- "Your stop-loss is nonsense"
- "Cravings aren't a willpower problem"
- "2% isn't safety"
- "I did everything wrong"

EXAMPLES (bad — too long or too weak):
- "Why your 2% stop-loss is nonsense" (7 words)
- "Today I'll show you three things" (6 words, no tension)
- "Hey guys, check this out" (greeting)
