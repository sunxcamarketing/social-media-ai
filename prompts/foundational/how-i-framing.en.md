# "HOW I" BEATS "HOW TO": Proof Over Claim

A core framing principle for hooks, titles, and ideas. Default mix: **70% "How I / Here's what I did"**, **30% "How to / How you"**: and the latter NEVER as a hook, only inside as a tactical step-through.

## THE PRINCIPLE

**"How I …" / "Here's what I did …" / "This is how my clients …"** = built-in social proof. You DID it. It happened. Story, authority, non-copyable.

**"How to …" / "How you can …" / "Here's how to …"** = claim. Sounds like a tutorial, course, generic teacher. Anyone who's read a book can say it.

Proof-based hooks almost always outperform pure teaching hooks. Story beats lesson plan: higher watchtime, more emotion, more "I want that too".

## COMPARISON

| ❌ Teacher (How-to) | ✅ Proof (How-I) |
|---|---|
| "How to build a content system with AI" | "How I built a weekly content machine with AI that fills my calendar" |
| "How to get more reach with Reels" | "How I went from chaotic posting to a 12-week AI content engine" |
| "How to use AI for content" | "How I replaced my content agency with a single AI workflow" |
| "5 tips for better scripts" | "What I stopped doing so my scripts would finally perform" |

## RULES

1. **HOOK & TITLE**: Default to proof framing. Prefer "How I", "Here's what I did", "What I learned", "What we did", "This is how my clients", "What happened when I".
2. **NEVER "How to"/"How you" as a hook**: that's commodity content. If the content has to be a tutorial, reframe the tutorial as personal experience.
3. **"How to"/"How you" is fine INSIDE the script**: as a tactical part after the proof hook. ("Here's how I did it: and here's how you can build the same.")
4. **Topics without your own experience**: Shift to a client's experience ("Here's what one of my clients did") or a concrete observation ("I noticed that …"). Never fall back to "How to".
5. **Weekly mix**: At most 1-2 pure "How to" scripts per week. The rest runs on proof framing.

## WHY THIS MATTERS FOR THE BRAND

The feed is full of "How to …" tutorials. That's interchangeable. Only YOU can say what YOU did, screwed up, changed. Your own experience can't be copied. "How I" sells YOU as the one solution. "How to" sells only information: and turns you into the next tutor voice in the scroll.
