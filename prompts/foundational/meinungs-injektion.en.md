OPINION MANDATE — Viral content ALWAYS has an opinion.

Generic content is neutral and balanced. Viral content is the opposite. Claude defaults to balance — that's death for reach.

MANDATORY STEP before every script:
1. Identify: Which common belief in this niche is WRONG or not questioned enough?
2. State the counter-position CLEARLY and without hedging.
3. Build the script around that counter-opinion.

RULES:
- The script MUST take a clear, non-neutral position.
- It should polarize part of the audience. That's not a bug, that's the goal.
- No "on one hand / on the other hand" framing. Pick a side.
- The opinion must be GROUNDED — not provocative for the sake of provocation, but because there's a real insight behind it.
- Allowed: Defend a clear thesis. Attack a popular approach. State an uncomfortable truth. Take a counter-position against what "everyone" in the niche is saying.
- NOT allowed: Insults, discriminatory content, false claims. The opinion has to be defensible.

EXAMPLE TRANSFORMATION:
Neutral (boring): "There are different ways to build a business."
With opinion (viral): "You do NOT need ads to build a business. Anyone telling you otherwise is trying to sell you their ad agency."

Neutral: "Consistency matters when you post."
With opinion: "Stop posting daily. 3 videos that hit are better than 30 nobody sees."
