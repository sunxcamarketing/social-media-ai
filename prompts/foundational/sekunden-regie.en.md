SECONDS-BASED DIRECTION — Scripts are thought of in seconds, not paragraphs.

A Reel is not a blog post. Every second counts. The script MUST be structured in time blocks:

## SHORT SCRIPT (30-40 seconds):
[Sec. 0-3] VISUAL HOOK — What does the viewer see before a word is spoken? What's in frame?
[Sec. 0-3] TEXT HOOK — First sentence. Max 8-12 words. No greeting, no intro.
[Sec. 3-8] OPEN LOOP — A claim or question that is NOT resolved right away.
[Sec. 8-25] BODY — 2-3 points, each with a micro pattern interrupt (surprising number, counter-example, quick perspective shift).
[Sec. 25-33] RE-HOOK — Callback to the open loop. "And now back to the question from the start..."
[Sec. 33-40] PAYOFF + CTA — Resolution + one single call-to-action.

## LONG SCRIPT (60+ seconds):
[Sec. 0-3] VISUAL HOOK — What does the viewer see before a word is spoken?
[Sec. 0-3] TEXT HOOK — First sentence. Max 8-12 words.
[Sec. 3-10] OPEN LOOP — Stronger version: Why does the viewer have to stay NOW?
[Sec. 10-45] BODY — 3-4 points, each with a micro pattern interrupt. Every 15 seconds an energy spike (rhetorical question, surprising twist, direct address).
[Sec. 45-55] RE-HOOK — Callback to the open loop. Close the tension arc.
[Sec. 55-65] PAYOFF + CTA — Resolution + call-to-action.

## RULES:
- Seconds markers are MANDATORY in the output. Every block is labeled with [Sec. X-Y].
- No block without a clear purpose. If a block brings no new info or emotion → cut it.
- Every 15 seconds a "micro pattern interrupt" has to hit — otherwise the viewer loses interest.
- The RE-HOOK at seconds 45-55 is NOT optional. It's the reason viewers stick around until the end.
