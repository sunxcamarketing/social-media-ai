# STORYTELLING FORMULA FOR VIDEO CONTENT

## The Core Principle

The best stories in the world (Pixar, Marvel, Breaking Bad) all work on the same principle: an ordinary person is forced by a crisis to change. Not because they want to. Because they have to.

We don't follow heroes who are strong. We follow heroes who are vulnerable and keep going anyway.

## The Pixar Formula

"Once upon a time… Every day… Until one day… Because of that… Until finally… And ever since then…"

It's the simplest and most powerful storytelling structure there is.

## Hitchcock's Suspense Principle

Surprise lasts 5 seconds. Suspense lasts 5 minutes. The difference is whether the viewer knows there's a bomb under the table.

Translated to social media: show the viewer at the start where the story is headed. Not the payoff, but the direction. "I lost everything and then…" The viewer now knows: a comeback is coming. They keep watching because they want to know HOW.

## The Zeigarnik Effect (Netflix Principle)

Unfinished things stick in memory longer than finished ones. Every episode ends with an open question. Your brain can't switch off because it wants resolution.

In a script: the hook promises something, and every following sentence is a mini-promise that gets paid off before the next one lands. The viewer is always chasing a small promise. You never give it all at once. You give just enough to keep them there.

## The WICK Formula (Core Framework for Storytelling Scripts)

### W — Wound
Show the pain. CONCRETE, not vague. Not "I was going through a tough time" but "I was in Germany, three rejections in one week, laptop open, no clue what comes next". The viewer has to be able to SEE the scene. The more concrete the scene, the stronger the emotion.

### I — Identity Shift
The moment something changed. Not slowly, but a specific instant. A realization, a conversation, a decision. Without a shift it's not a story, it's a report.

### C — Cost
What did the change cost? What did you have to let go of, give up, accept? This is the emotional core. People don't just want to see the success, they want to know the price. That's what makes it believable.

### K — Key Lesson
ONE. Not three. Not five. One insight the viewer walks away with. It has to be so concrete you could explain it to someone in a single sentence.

## Why Vulnerability Works

It comes down to mirror neurons. When you show pain, the same neurons fire in the viewer's brain as if they were experiencing the pain themselves. They feel it with you. And what we feel with, we follow. Perfection doesn't trigger mirror neurons. Vulnerability does.

## Critical Rule: Spend More Time in the Pain

The story needs more time in the pain (W and C) before it moves to the lesson (K). Don't jump straight to the lesson. Let the viewer feel the pain with you first. The lesson comes later.
