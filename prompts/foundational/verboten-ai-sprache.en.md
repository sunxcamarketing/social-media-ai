FORBIDDEN — Typical AI Language
If you write any of these phrases, the script has failed.
No synonyms or slight variations either.

- "Many people..."
- "Imagine this..."
- "In today's world..."
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "In a world where..."

- "Did you know that..."

- "More and more people are asking..."

- "Let's..."  (as script opener)
- "Let's dive in..." / "Let's dive into..."
- "I'm going to take you on a journey..."
- "It's time to..."
- "Picture this..."
- "Ready? Here we go."
- "Buckle up."
- "Hold onto your hats..."

## 10. Rhetorical one-word questions (THE worst AI tell)
- "The result?", "The kicker?", "The best part?"
- "The difference?", "The solution?", "My advice?"
- "The lesson?", "What happened?", "The point?"
- "The truth?", "The problem?", "The catch?"
- "The twist?", "The secret?", "The bottom line?"

## 11. Empty authority phrases
- "It's important to note that..."
- "It's worth mentioning that..."
- "It goes without saying that..."
- "There's no denying that..."
- "Many experts agree that..."
- "This leads to the realization that..."
- "A great way to achieve this is..."
- "Studies have shown..." (without citing the study)
- "Research suggests..." (vague)

## 12. Pretentious fragments & patterns
NOT: "Innovation. Disruption. Transformation. That's what defines success."
NOT: Three adjectives after an em-dash ("— secure, guaranteed, and undeniable")
NOT: Every sentence on its own line with a blank line (= obvious AI formatting tell)
INSTEAD: Concrete. Specific. The way a real person would actually talk.

## 13. Em-dashes / en-dashes
- NO em-dashes (—) or en-dashes (–) as a stylistic device. Huge AI tell.
- NOT: "Weight loss — but done right." or "Your body — your temple."
- Write normal sentences. Period. New sentence.

## 14. "Delve into" / "Dive into" / "Unpack"
- "Let's delve into..." / "Let's dive deeper..."
- "We need to unpack this." / "Let's unpack..."
- No real human talks like this. Say "let's look at" or "here's what's happening."

## 15. "This signals..." / "This underscores..."
- "This signals a shift in..." / "This underscores the need for..."
- That's AI connecting two ideas without having an actual opinion. A real person says: "People are doing X, so we have to change Y."

## 16. "Navigating the complexities" / "In an ever-changing landscape"
- "In an ever-changing world..." / "Navigating the complexities of X..."
- "In an ever-evolving landscape..."
- Says absolutely nothing. Which complexities? What's changing? Be specific.

## 17. "Leverage synergies" / "Holistic approach" / "Empower growth"
- "Unlock synergies" / "Leverage our learnings" / "Take a holistic approach"
- Corporate drivel. Sounds deep, means nothing. A real person says: "This worked, this didn't, so we're changing this."

## 18. Reddit-style filler
- "Not gonna lie..." / "Ngl..."
- "Low-key..." / "High-key..."
- "It hits different."
- "Periodt." / "Period."
- "No cap."
- "Me, personally..."
- "So, yeah."
- These read as ChatGPT trying to sound Gen-Z.

## 19. "Hear me out" / "Stay with me"
- "Hear me out..."
- "Stay with me here..."
- "Bear with me..."
- "Trust the process."
- Filler that buys time instead of delivering value.

## 20. "Make no mistake" / "Mark my words"
- "Make no mistake..."
- "Mark my words..."
- "Believe you me..."
- Empty authority. Just make the claim.

## 21. "Not just X, but Y" rhetorical structure
- "This isn't just about losing weight — it's about reclaiming your life."
- "It's not just a habit, it's a lifestyle."
- The "not just X, but Y" contrast is the #1 AI sentence pattern. Avoid entirely.

## 22. "— and here's why"
- "X is broken — and here's why."
- "This matters — and I'll tell you why."
- Just tell them why. Don't announce that you're about to.
