## The 4-Step Framework

### Step 1: RESEARCH (Doom-Scrolling with Intent)
- Open Instagram/TikTok and scroll the For You / Explore page
- Look for videos that match BOTH your **niche** AND your **format**
- Specifically look for **outliers**: videos with 5,000-10,000+ likes
- That's the indicator a video is viral and proven by the algorithm
- Check if it's an outlier for that specific creator (e.g., 1M views on a 68K follower account)
- The algorithm optimizes for **watch time**, not post times or hashtags

### Step 2: SCRIPTING (Copy → Adapt → Simplify)
- Open a Google Doc and start the scripting process
- Write down the original creator's sentences line by line
- Under each line, write YOUR version in YOUR niche
- **Don't reinvent the wheel** — find a proven viral concept and rewrite it in your own words
- Think critically about **WHY** it went viral (psychology of the viewer)
- Empathize with your viewer — who is your audience? What resonates with beginners?

**Hook Rules:**
- The hook = first sentence = make or break
- Must capture attention or viewers scroll instantly
- Appeal to wants and desires of your ideal viewer
- Every single sentence must tie back to the hook

**Simplification:**
- Dumb down concepts for 60-second video
- Don't try to prove expertise with complex jargon
- A 5-year-old should understand it
- Short form = one quick dopamine hit, not a PhD dissertation
- Condense value so viewer feels they understand ONE thing

**Revision Process:**
- Read the ENTIRE script aloud, word for word
- Check flow — at no point should it feel robotic or confusing
- Ask: "If I were a stranger watching this, would I scroll off at any point?"
- Reasons to scroll: confused, bored, stopped believing
- Inject dopamine hits — make viewer feel like they're vicariously winning

**Key Insight on Value:**
- Most people save content but don't actually apply it
- Index harder for making them FEEL like they're making progress
- Don't try to teach PhD-level concepts in 30 seconds
- Get them one quick win or one quick feeling of understanding

### Step 3: FILMING (iPhone + CapCut Teleprompter)

**Pre-filming ritual:**
- Get into high energy state (energy drink, whatever works for you)
- Stretch and deep breaths to calm nervous system
- Don't show up rigid/awkward on camera
- Noise-cancelling headphones + focus music (Brain FM or meditation music)
- Context switch from business brain to charismatic-camera-person brain

**Teleprompter hack:**
- Open script on phone → copy text
- Open CapCut → Camera → flip camera → 3 minutes → Teleprompter
- Paste script into teleprompter
- Goal: make it seem like you're NOT reading a script
- Bring natural energy, not exaggerated words
- Visualize somebody in front of you
- Make viewer feel like they're talking to a real human

**Recording:**
- Film on iPhone, no fancy equipment needed
- Stack phone on books for desk shot
- Don't need to nail it in one take — mess up, keep recording
- Cut mistakes in editing

### Step 4: EDITING (CapCut on iPhone)

**4-part editing process:**
1. **Rough cut** — remove dead space, cut mistakes (split + delete)
2. **B-roll** — overlay clips that match what you're saying
3. **Auto captions** — TikTok Classic font, press return so 2 words per line
4. **Music** — instrumental only (no lyrics competing with speech)

**B-roll philosophy:**
- Plan a shot list from your script BEFORE filming B-roll
- Each sentence = a specific visual
- Think: what makes this sentence EASIER to understand visually?
- Screen recordings for app/process shots
- Crop overlays/stickers for UI elements (don't show full complex UI)
- Speed up boring parts (typing, scrolling) by 3-5x
- Keep visual simplicity — viewer should focus on ONE thing per shot
- Every B-roll clip should be as short as possible (1-2 seconds)

**Dead space rule:**
- Cut ALL breathing pauses, word trips, silence
- Even 0.5 seconds of dead space makes video feel slower
- Especially important for newer creators — be vigilant

**5% intentionality principle:**
- 5% more intentionality per sentence = potentially 10x in views
- Small incremental retention differences compound massively
- Think about WHY each visual exists

**Social proof:**
- Add screenshots of view counts in first few seconds
- Crop to just show the numbers (no clutter)
- Proves you're worth listening to

**Captions:**
- Use TikTok Classic font specifically
- Make sure eyes are visible (don't block face with text)
- Yellow captions if background needs contrast
- Press return so each caption fits in one line (2 words at a time)

**Music:**
- Instrumental only — no lyrics competing with speech
- Download via YTMP3 (YouTube to MP3 converter)
- In CapCut: Sounds → Device → search song
- Match music mood to video mood (casual/funny for casual video)

**Export settings:**
- 4K resolution
- Do NOT use AI Ultra HD
- 30 fps frame rate
- Recommended bit rate

**Caption/posting:**
- Caption doesn't matter much (small on screen)
- No hashtags or posting times matter
- Can reiterate steps as caption for extra engagement/watch time

## Key Psychological Principles

1. **Algorithm = Watch Time** — Not hashtags, not post times. Just which videos get the most watch time and engagement.
2. **Proven > Original** — Copy proven viral structures. Don't try to be original from scratch, especially as a beginner.
3. **Empathize with Viewer** — Think through their lens. What would make THEM scroll off? What would make THEM excited?
4. **Visual Simplicity** — Every frame should have ONE focus point. Don't clutter the screen.
5. **Progressive Value** — Each sentence should deliver new information. It should get better, not worse.
6. **Dopamine Hits** — Make the viewer feel like they're winning/progressing just by watching.
7. **Speed = Value Perception** — Fast-changing visuals make content feel value-dense and worth saving.
8. **Social Proof** — Show results early to establish credibility.
9. **One Quick Win** — Short form isn't about deep education. It's about ONE feeling of understanding.
10. **5% Intentionality** — Small improvements in each sentence/visual compound to 10x view differences.
